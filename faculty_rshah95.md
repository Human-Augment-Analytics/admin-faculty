# Faculty Onboarding + Faculty <-> Student Relations
Rohil Shah
## Problem
Onboarding for faculty for HAAG research is currently captured in a lengthy document on a GT sites page. The document is likely out of date in some aspects and also too verbose for sharing with prospective faculty. Similarly there is no formal documentation capturing faculty to student relations and interactions. This leaves onboarding unclear and potential disengagement.

## Initiative Goal
Design and validate a streamlined faculty onboarding artifact and a formal faculty ↔ student relations framework that reduces time-to-engagement for new faculty affiliates and establishes clear expectations for both parties throughout the project lifecycle.

## Initiatives
1. Project Contract Initiation — The process by which a new or returning faculty collaborator meets with a HAAG project manager to define project scope, designate a point-of-contact, and produce a 1–2 page formal contract covering scope of work, researcher count, communication preferences, data conditions, and resources.
2. Student–Faculty Introduction & Communication Setup — The handoff procedure where an assigned student researcher is formally introduced to the faculty collaborator and communication channels are established according to the collaborator's preferred structure.
3. Advisor–Faculty Introduction & Communication Setup — The handoff procedure where an assigned computational advisor is formally introduced to the faculty collaborator and communication channels are established according to the collaborator's preferred structure.

## Collecting Information
- Understand current project manager–faculty contract initiation meetings to document what information is solicited, in what order, and where gaps or confusion arise.
- Interview current and past faculty affiliates using a structured protocol to understand their first 30 days in HAAG — what was clear, what was not, and what resources they actually used.
- Interview student researchers across project stages to capture the faculty ↔ student dynamic from the student perspective — communication frequency, clarity of expectations, and feedback quality.
- Interview computational advisors across project stages to capture the faculty ↔ advisor dynamic from the advisory perspective — communication frequency, clarity of expectations, and feedback quality.
- Audit the existing GT Sites FATE page and any linked contract templates to catalog what is documented, what is outdated, and what is missing relative to actual practice.

## Resources
- HAAG GT Sites FATE Page
- Existing project contract template/one-pages (if exists)
- HAAG Advisors/students (current and former)
- HAAG ACM and IEEE publications
- Comparable lab frameworks outside HAAG

## Validation
- Pilot the revised onboarding artifact with one incoming faculty affiliate and collect structured feedback on clarity, completeness, and accuracy after their first project contract is signed.
- Track measurable proxies: Time from first faculty inquiry to signed contract, number of follow-up clarification requests from faculty during onboarding, and student-reported clarity on faculty expectations at project kickoff.
