# Onboarding / Project Showcase for HAAG
Drafted by Kevin Lemus-Medrano, CS8803
# Procedure Topic: Faculty Relations
Standardized Faculty Engagement and Retention Framework

# Problem Statement
Currently, faculty engagement within HAAG lacks a consistent structure. There is no formal procedure for how often managers or project leads should communicate with faculty sponsors, no standardized method for collecting faculty feedback, and no documented process for setting expectations around involvement. This leads to unclear communication frequency, faculty disengagement, and lower semester-to-semester retention.

# Initiative Goal

The goal of this initiative is to design and evaluate a standardized faculty engagement procedure that improves communication consistency and retention across HAAG. Deliverables will include a faculty check-in template, a feedback survey, and a recommended communication schedule for managers and PMs.
Initiative Framework

1. Discovery
To understand the current state of faculty engagement, I will review the master Excel sheet to compare last semester's faculty sponsors against current sponsors to identify the retention rate. I will also observe communication patterns in Slack and directly communicate with current faculty to understand their experience and concerns.
2. Hypothesis
If HAAG adopts a standardized faculty engagement procedure that includes a defined communication schedule, a structured check-in template, and a semester feedback survey, then faculty retention will improve and communication gaps between HAAG admin and faculty will be reduced.
3. Experiment
Because a full semester cycle is needed to measure long-term retention, this initiative will use a pilot implementation and survey-based evaluation.
Develop a standardized communication schedule defining when and how managers should check in with faculty (e.g., bi-weekly Slack message, monthly async update). Create a faculty check-in template covering project status, faculty concerns, involvement satisfaction, and support needed. Design a short mid-semester feedback survey. Pilot the procedure with a small group of faculty sponsors, then distribute the survey to both the pilot group and a control group. Compare responses to evaluate effectiveness.
4. Metrics for Evaluation
The evaluation will focus on faculty satisfaction scores, clarity of expectations as reported by faculty, consistency of communication touchpoints, and comparison of engagement between pilot and control groups.
5. Report & Iterate
Following the pilot, identified gaps will be documented, templates will be revised based on feedback, and a finalized faculty engagement procedure will be produced for future semesters.
Intended Audience
This procedure is intended for future HAAG managers and PMs, HAAG admin, and team leads who interface with faculty sponsors.
Organizational Need Being Served
This initiative serves the need for consistent faculty communication, improved retention, clear expectations for both faculty and HAAG leadership, and a documented process that serves as a manual for incoming managers.
