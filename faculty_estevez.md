## Problem Statement

Within HAAG, research projects involve collaboration between researchers, faculty sponsors, computational advisors, and research managers. However, there is currently no structured system that clearly documents the research expertise, technical skills, or prior project experience of participants across these roles.

As a result, project assignments and collaborations are often formed through informal communication or ad-hoc matching rather than through a clear understanding of available expertise. This can make it difficult for researchers to identify faculty or advisors aligned with their interests and for project managers to assign teams that best match the research objectives of a project.

Improving visibility into the expertise and experience of researchers, faculty, and computational advisors could enable more effective project alignment and allow participants to either be assigned to, or self-select into, research initiatives where their skills and interests are most relevant.

---

## Stakeholders

This initiative involves four primary stakeholders:

- **Researchers** – Primary contributors to research projects who benefit from clearer visibility into faculty expertise and project opportunities.
- **Faculty Sponsors** – Provide subject-matter expertise, research direction, and collaboration opportunities.
- **Computational Advisors** – Provide technical and methodological guidance across projects.
- **Research Managers** – Coordinate project operations and help align researchers, advisors, and faculty sponsors with appropriate research initiatives.

Researchers are expected to be the **primary users of the proposed system**, as improved visibility into expertise will help them better align with projects and collaborators.

---

## Initiative Goal

The goal of this initiative is to design and evaluate a **Research Expertise Visibility and Alignment Framework** for HAAG.

This framework will create a structured way to document the research interests, expertise, and prior project involvement of researchers, faculty sponsors, and computational advisors. The objective is to improve transparency of expertise across the organization and enable better alignment between research projects and participants.

---

## Initiative Framework

### 1. Discovery

To understand the current state of research alignment within HAAG, I will:

- Review existing project records and the HAAG master tracking spreadsheet to identify researchers, faculty sponsors, and advisors associated with past and current projects.
- Observe communication patterns within HAAG Slack channels.
- Speak with researchers and project managers to understand how project assignments and collaborations currently occur.

---

### 2. Hypothesis

If HAAG implements a structured system that documents the expertise and research interests of researchers, faculty sponsors, and computational advisors, then project alignment will improve and participants will be able to more easily identify and engage in research projects that match their expertise.

---

### 3. Experiment

A prototype expertise directory will be created that includes profiles for:

- researchers
- faculty sponsors
- computational advisors

Each profile may include:

- research interests
- technical skills
- prior HAAG project involvement
- collaboration interests

This directory will be tested during project assignment or team formation processes to determine whether it improves alignment between projects and participants.

---

## Metrics for Evaluation

The evaluation will focus on:

- researcher ability to identify projects aligned with their expertise
- clarity and usefulness of expertise information
- satisfaction of faculty and advisors with project alignment
- feedback from research managers on ease of assigning teams

---

## Report & Iterate

Following the pilot implementation, feedback from researchers, faculty sponsors, computational advisors, and research managers will be collected.

Based on this feedback, the structure of the expertise directory and alignment process will be refined with the goal of establishing a standardized framework for improving collaboration and project alignment within HAAG.
